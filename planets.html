Sun

The center of the Solar System, a massive ball of hot gas that provides light and heat to all the planets.

Mercury

The closest planet to the Sun, small, and with extreme temperature changes due to its thin atmosphere.

Venus

Known for its thick atmosphere of carbon dioxide, Venus has surface temperatures hot enough to melt lead.

Earth

The only known planet with life, featuring liquid water, a breathable atmosphere, and diverse ecosystems.

Mars

Known as the "Red Planet" due to its iron oxide-rich surface, it has the tallest volcano and deepest canyon in the Solar System.

Jupiter

The largest planet, a gas giant with a famous Great Red Spot, which is a massive storm, and dozens of moons.

Saturn

Famous for its beautiful ring system, made of ice and rock particles, Saturn is also a gas giant.

Uranus

An ice giant with a unique feature of rotating on its side, creating extreme seasons.

Neptune

The farthest planet from the Sun, with a striking blue color caused by methane in its atmosphere and strong winds.

Pluto

Once considered the ninth planet, now classified as a dwarf planet, it has an elliptical orbit and is located in the Kuiper Belt.
